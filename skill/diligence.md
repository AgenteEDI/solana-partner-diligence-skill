# Diligence Workflow

## Evidence to collect

- product surface and primary use case
- docs quality and maintenance signals
- repository activity and ownership visibility
- security claims and whether they are specific
- dependency footprint and blast radius
- onboarding complexity and support burden
- pricing, limits, or hidden commercial constraints

## Review dimensions

### 1. Product clarity

- is the product easy to explain in one sentence
- is the integration surface clear
- are usage constraints explicit

### 2. Reliability and trust

- is the team visible
- are incidents acknowledged
- is there evidence of active maintenance

### 3. Technical coupling

- what parts of our stack would depend on this
- can we swap it out later
- what are the fallback paths if it degrades

### 4. Security posture

- are they precise or vague about security
- do they expose sensible failure boundaries
- are they asking for unsafe privileges or opaque binaries

### 5. Business reality

- do incentives align
- is there a clear reason they want the partnership
- do we gain leverage or just more work

## Output behavior

- distinguish observed evidence from inference
- do not confuse polished docs with low risk
- if evidence is weak, say so plainly
