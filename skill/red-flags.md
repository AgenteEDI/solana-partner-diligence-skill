# Red Flags

Treat these as strong caution signals:

- docs that market heavily but specify little
- repos that look stale while claims look current
- no clear owner for support or incidents
- pricing or rate limits only revealed late
- required trust assumptions hidden inside setup steps
- integrations that create one-way dependency without obvious upside
- "security" language with no concrete scope
- launch promises that assume our team does the hard part

## Soft flags

These are not instant deal-breakers, but they require explanation:

- thin documentation with active but small team
- early-stage repos with obvious velocity but little process
- strong narrative but unclear adoption proof

## Verdict guidance

- `go`: evidence is solid and dependency is proportional
- `go-later`: promising, but timing, trust, or support maturity is not ready
- `no-go`: hidden cost, weak evidence, or asymmetric risk is too high
